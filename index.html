Course Information

Name: Fundamentals of Accounting

Code: ACCT207

Description: This course covers accounting from the user perspective through the introduction of financial statements and the examination of the interrelationships among them. Through real-world business examples, students examine key accounting topics including receivables, inventory, plant assets, liabilities, and stock transactions (and the effects on the financial statements). Financial statement analysis is presented through the evaluation of the profitability, liquidity, and solvency of businesses.

Prerequisites: N/A

Course Objectives

  • Weeks 1 and 8: Identify the key financial statements and their components and analyze the financial performance of a company through various financial statement analysis techniques.
  • Week 2: Analyze business transactions within the framework of the accounting equation contributed capital (stocks).
  • Week 3: Analyze business transactions within the framework of the accounting equation related to current assets.
  • Week 4: Analyze business transactions within the framework of the accounting equation related to long-lived assets.
  • Week 5: Analyze business transactions within the framework of the accounting equation related to liabilities.
  • Week 6: Analyze business transactions within the framework of the accounting equation earned capital (revenue and expenses).
  • Week 7: Analyze the effects of merchandising and inventory transactions on the financial statements of a company.
  • Week 8: Analyze business performance using financial statement ratios.
